Remarkable Bright Creatures

Sheldy Van Belt

Remarkable Bright Creatures by Shelby Van Pelt is a heartwarming and quirky debut novel that explores themes of grief, loneliness, and unexpected connections. The story revolves around Tova Sullivan, a widowed woman in her seventies who works the night shift at a small aquarium in the Pacific Northwest. Tova has lived a life of solitude since the death of her husband, and her only companion is a grumpy octopus named Marcellus. Marcellus, who has lived in the aquarium for years, is highly intelligent and observant. He begins to form an unlikely bond with Tova, offering her a sense of companionship and understanding that she hasn’t felt in years. The novel alternates between Tova’s perspective and Marcellus’s, whose sharp wit and observations provide much of the novel’s humor and insight. As the story unfolds, Tova’s life begins to intersect with the lives of others in her community, including a young man named Ethan, who is struggling with his own sense of belonging. Together, they uncover secrets from Tova’s past and face difficult truths about themselves and their relationships. Remarkable Bright Creatures is a beautifully written exploration of human connection, the complexities of aging, and the unexpected ways in which we heal from grief. The book’s tender prose, memorable characters, and delightful portrayal of Marcellus make it a deeply emotional and uplifting read.
